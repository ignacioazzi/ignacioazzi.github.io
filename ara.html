Developer Console for Modular Phone

Creation of the developer console, a tool by which developers register and manage modules.

Project overview

Two types of users worked together in the Developer Console to fullfill its main objective: The creation, approval, publication and management of each module, which would then be ready for sale in the Module Store.

Module makers would register and manage their modules, while Google Admins would review their information, and approve or reject each module.

My job as a UX Designer in the project

As I was not involved in the project from start, my first objective as the UX designer was to generate an expert review to mark pain points and propose solutions to create a more standardized, scalable UX. The process of registration, approval, and publication of each module was the most important operation within the DC. The analysis and redesign of that user flow took the most part of my time spent in the project. Its importance could be explained by 3 points:

  • Multiple users interact at different stages of the process.
  • It was a long process with a big load of information to be filled.
  • That information was going to be consumed by the finals users in the Module Store.

Due to the complexity of user tasks and flows, I generated user testings to test my UX assumptions. I worked closely with the visual designer to ensure that our design were material design compliant. Finally, as the project was in development and decisions changed rapidly, we kept weekly meetings with the client partner and the client himself to share our progress and understand their requirements.

Main layout changes

  1. Stepper Steppers display progress through the module creation sequence by breaking it up into multiple logical and numbered steps.
  2. Two column layout The helper text column aids the user in how to fill the inputs field. It also communicates when filling a field is required for approval.
  3. Navigational buttonsLet the user moving forward and backward in the process.
  4. Module status bar Communicates the module status and explains what to do to continue to the next step.
  5. Status bar actions Display up to 2 of the most important actions available.
