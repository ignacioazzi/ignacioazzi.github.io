My job at Educabilia

Whiteboard + Sketching + Illustrator/Photoshop + Sublime Text

We use the SCRUM framework, splitting our tasks into sprints. We had daily design meetings where we started sketching on a whiteboard for each task. This allowed us to work together, designing a consistent user flow.

When we reached a consensus, I moved on to design the wireframes in Illustrator. Once our own set of UI components were created, we usually sketched it out and then jumped directly to the browser.

We used semantic HTML5 for the markup, Sass + Compass for the visual styling, We also based our CSS architecture on concepts from SMACSS.

Course Page

One of the most complex page to design as it showed a lot of different information.

  • Image, title and subtitle at top.

    Image size was choosen so both title and subtitle could use 2 lines each, based on the lenghty course title we had.

  • Then the "info bar"

    It showed the most relevant details like price, course type, location and timetable. Our research showed that those 4 pieces of information were the key to whether somebody would take a course or not, so it needed to be easily understandable and in a good spot.

  • Below, the main content.

    Splitted in tabs as a way to keep a consistent look across the different courses description and to help course creators to organize their info and keep them easily findable.

Social Media imagery

Image produced for courses highlighted in Facebook Twitter and Instagram

Educabilia users had the possibility to showcase their courses in the platform social network accounts by paying an extra fee.

As it was one of my many tasks I designed them by using one size that could fit in the different social network dimensions.

Special care was put in having a safe zone where course´s title should fit as Twitter preview images by hiding a part of their top and bottom.
