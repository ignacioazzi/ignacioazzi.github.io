Saving for Gamers

Quick Save is a unique tool that give gameplayers the control that they want in terms of understanding where their money is being invested. Its tailored specifically to the games market, so transactions are plain, simple and rewarding.

Project Overview

The main objective of the project was to research the gaming market and gamers and discover whether there was a feasible business model where millennials would be encouraged to save and invest by using a product from our client.

The client

The client is a financial and investment company who was looking to research and ideate a business model that would encourage millennials to improve their saving and investment habits through the aid of gaming.

How we addressed the project

We traveled to New York where we met our client to understand his business model, current situation, objectives, other projects, and why he was pitching to millennials. The UX team ideated a series of interviews, conducted by me, to understand millennials needs, objectives, motivations, and savings/gaming habits.
Based on the market and user research, we proceed to gather our finding and participate in an internal ideation where we proposed lots of ideas. Those ideas were organized, categorized, and voted on. Three ideas were presented to the client, and the one picked was then developed into the final product ideation.

My role in the project

I was part of the UX team, working both as a researcher, conducting the user interviews, and as a designer, ideating the final product UX.

User research

Who we interviewed and what we found

The user profile was US millenials gamers who were employed, about to get a degree or who already got one.

User interview objectives

Ethnographic interviews were fundamental to be able to identify user needs, expectations, problems and motivations related to saving habits and investments. We are also interested in learning about their saving goals and habits.

Interview script main topics

  • Career and job field.
  • Amount of money spent in games.
  • Thoughts and plans about their future.
  • Existance and opinions about had student debts.
  • Savings: Whether they save, habits and methods.
  • Investments: Whether the invest and how: Through applications, manual methods.
  • Use of desktop and mobile apps, what types of features they would like it to have. As he imagined the ideal application.

Insight analysis

After interviewing 10 users, analyzing and categorizing their responses, we were able to define 3 different profiles of users, with their similarities and differences. Our research determined that the different profiles found were directly related to the different stages of life that each one traversed
